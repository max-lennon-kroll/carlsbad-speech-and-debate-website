Our Courses

Oral Interpretation

Also known as “Beginning Speech and Debate,” this course concentrates on the development, improvement, and practice of public speaking, acting, and argumentation skills. Students discuss current events, practice research and writing skills, and deliver presentations including prepared speaking; as well as impromptu (on-the-spot) and short-preparation speaking situations, including debates. These activities are designed to improve abilities and confidence in all aspects of public presentation. This class is required to be on the Carlsbad Speech and Debate team.

Advanced Speaking

This course offers extensive exploration of persuasive and informative public speaking as well as the interpretation of literature. Students will study and practice writing and delivery techniques of various types of speeches in preparation for their own presentations with the goal of competing on the CHS Speech and Debate Team. Competition on the team is used for performance finals throughout the class.

Advanced Debating

This course offers extensive study of style and skills for argumentation, research, critical thinking along with current events. Students participate in extemporaneous speaking and specific types of debate: Public Forum, Parliamentary, Lincoln Douglas and Congressional Debate with the goal of competing on the CHS Speech and Debate Team. Competition on the team is used for performance finals throughout the class.
